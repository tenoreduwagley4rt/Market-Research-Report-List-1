<p><p>The FOUP ID Reader market comprises two primary types: Low-Frequency (LF) RFID Readers and High-Frequency (HF) RFID Readers. LF RFID Readers operate typically at 125 kHz to 134 kHz, offering advantages in close-range applications, such as tracking wafers in semiconductor environments. Conversely, HF RFID Readers, functioning around 13.56 MHz, provide enhanced read distances and data transfer speeds, making them suitable for applications requiring more robust data management. Both types serve crucial roles in automating inventory and improving efficiency in semiconductor manufacturing processes.</p></p>
